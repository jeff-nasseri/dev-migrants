## **Overview: Two-Phase Immigration Path**

**Phase 1:** Startup Visa (1 year) → **Phase 2:** Self-Employment Residence Permit (renewable)

---

## **PHASE 1: STARTUP VISA APPLICATION**

### **Step 1: Develop Your Business Plan** (PRIORITY: HIGH | Timeline: 2-4 weeks)

**What you need to do:**
- Draft a comprehensive business plan covering your innovative idea, market analysis, target customers, growth strategy, and financial projections
- Focus on demonstrating innovation - your software solution must be:
  - New to the Netherlands market, OR
  - Incorporate new technology for production/distribution, OR
  - Involve innovative organizational approach
- Include a detailed 12-month development roadmap
- Show scalability potential and economic benefit to Netherlands

**Key requirement:** The plan must clearly explain how you'll go from idea to operational company within one year.

---

### **Step 2: Secure Sufficient Financial Resources** (PRIORITY: HIGH | Timeline: Ongoing)

**What you need to prove:**
- Minimum €14,000 in accessible bank accounts (approximately 70% of Dutch minimum wage)
- Some sources recommend showing €17,000-€23,000 to be safe
- Must be verifiable and available without restrictions
- Bank statements should be dated within 1 month of application

---

### **Step 3: Find and Contract with a Recognized Facilitator** (PRIORITY: CRITICAL | Timeline: 2-6 weeks)

**What you need to do:**
- Review the official list of recognized facilitators on the RVO website or in Document 1
- Research facilitators specialized in tech/software startups
- Relevant options from the list include:
  - **CIC Rotterdam** - "Connecting innovators to make things happen"
  - **Erasmus Centre for Entrepreneurship** - Strong in tech
  - **TNW Amsterdam** - Tech-focused, part of Financial Times group
  - **UtrechtInc** - University-linked, focuses on tech startups
  - **Rockstart** - Global accelerator with follow-on funding

**Facilitator requirements:**
- Must have experience with innovative startups
- Financially stable (not bankrupt, no negative equity)
- Cannot have majority interest in your company
- Cannot be a family member (up to 3rd degree)
- Will provide tailored support: mentorship, operational management, marketing, research, investor connections

**What this costs:**
- Facilitator fees vary widely (€2,000-€10,000+ per year)
- Discuss fees upfront before signing agreement
- Most facilitators charge an additional fee for their services beyond the visa application fee

**Deliverable:** Signed facilitator agreement outlining their role and your partnership

---

### **Step 4: Prepare Required Documents** (PRIORITY: HIGH | Timeline: 2-3 weeks)

**Documents needed:**
- Valid passport (must be valid for entire stay period + 6 months)
- Completed IND application form
- Comprehensive business plan (approved by facilitator)
- Signed facilitator agreement
- Proof of financial means showing approximately €14,000 in accessible funds
- Health/travel insurance covering your stay
- Passport-sized photos (Dutch visa standards)
- Educational certificates (translated if needed)
- Any relevant work experience documentation

**Important:** Documents must be legalized (apostille if necessary) and translated into Dutch or English

---

### **Step 5: Submit Application & Pay Fees** (PRIORITY: HIGH | Timeline: 1 week)

**Application options:**

**As an Iranian citizen, you will need an MVV (provisional residence permit):**
- Apply at Dutch embassy/consulate in Iran (or your country of residence)
- You'll apply for both MVV and residence permit simultaneously
- Your facilitator CANNOT apply on your behalf if you need an MVV

**Costs:**
- IND application fee: €380-€405
- Facilitator fee: €2,000-€10,000+ (varies by facilitator)
- Document translation: €25-€50 per page
- Apostille/legalization: €80-€120 per document
- Health insurance: €600-€1,200 per year

**Total estimated first-year costs: €20,000-€25,000** (including living expenses)

---

### **Step 6: Wait for Decision** (PRIORITY: N/A - Processing Time | Timeline: Up to 90 days)

**What happens:**
- IND consults with Netherlands Enterprise Agency (RVO) to assess your application and business plan innovation
- RVO evaluates:
  - Innovation level of your product/service
  - Scalability potential
  - Economic benefit to Netherlands
  - Facilitator credibility
  - Your financial stability

**Processing time:** Typically 90 days maximum

---

### **Step 7: Collect Your Permit & Enter Netherlands** (PRIORITY: HIGH | Timeline: 2-4 weeks after approval)

**If approved:**
- Pick up your provisional residence permit (MVV) at Dutch embassy/consulate
- Travel to Netherlands
- Collect your temporary residence permit at IND office or Expat Center
- **Within first weeks in Netherlands:**
  - Register at local municipality (BRP - Municipal Personal Records Database)
  - Obtain BSN (citizen service number)
  - Open Dutch bank account
  - Register company with Chamber of Commerce (KVK) - requires residence permit
  - Apply for VAT number if needed
  - Arrange Dutch health insurance

---

## **PHASE 2: TRANSITION TO SELF-EMPLOYMENT PERMIT**

### **Step 8: Build Your Business During Year 1** (PRIORITY: CRITICAL | Timeline: 12 months)

**What you must do:**
- Develop and launch your innovative product/service
- Work closely with your facilitator
- Meet milestones outlined in your business plan
- Document progress, customer acquisition, revenue
- Build network and partnerships
- Consider hiring employees if needed

**Goal:** Demonstrate business viability and growth to qualify for self-employment permit

---

### **Step 9: Apply for Self-Employment Residence Permit** (PRIORITY: HIGH | Timeline: Month 9-11 of startup visa)

**When to apply:** 2-3 months before your startup visa expires

**Two pathways:**

**Pathway A: With Facilitator Declaration (EASIER)**
- If facilitator is satisfied with your progress, they sign a declaration that exempts you from the points system
- This allows you to get self-employment permit for 2 years
- Much simpler process

**Pathway B: Standard Points System (HARDER)**
- Required if facilitator doesn't provide declaration
- Must score minimum 90 points total across 3 categories (30 points minimum per category):
  1. **Personal experience** (education, entrepreneurship experience)
  2. **Business plan** (innovation, market potential, financial viability)
  3. **Added value to Netherlands** (innovation, employment creation, investments)
- Maximum 300 points possible
- Foreign diplomas must be assessed by Instituut voor Diploma Waardering (IDW) - start this process early as it takes time

**Requirements for self-employment permit:**
- Proof business serves essential Dutch economic interest
- Business is operational
- Sufficient income generation
- Valid business registration with KVK

---

## **KEY SUCCESS FACTORS & TIPS**

**1. Start early:** Begin preparation 4-6 months before intended move date

**2. Choose the right facilitator:**
- Research their track record with software startups
- Ask about success rates
- Understand exactly what support they provide
- Get clear fee structure upfront

**3. Make your innovation clear:**
- Don't just copy existing business models
- Show what makes your solution unique for Netherlands/Europe
- Demonstrate technical innovation or new market approach
- Use concrete examples and data

**4. Financial planning:**
- Budget €20,000-€25,000 for first year minimum
- Keep detailed financial records
- Maintain buffer beyond minimum €14,000

**5. Build relationships:**
- Network actively during your startup visa year
- Join Dutch tech/startup communities
- Attend meetups and events
- Build customer base early

**6. Documentation discipline:**
- Keep copies of everything
- Track all milestones and achievements
- Document customer traction, partnerships, revenue
- This helps with self-employment permit application

**7. Family considerations:**
- Yes, you can bring family members on dependent visas
- They need separate applications
- Additional financial requirements apply

---

## **IMPORTANT NOTES FOR IRANIAN CITIZENS**

- You WILL need an MVV (provisional residence permit) to enter Netherlands
- Apply at Dutch embassy/consulate
- Processing may take longer due to additional security checks
- Ensure all Iranian documents have apostille and certified translations
- Consider geopolitical factors that might affect processing times
