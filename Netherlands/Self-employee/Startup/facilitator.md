# Startup Visa Facilitators Guide

## What is a Facilitator?

A **facilitator** (also called a **business mentor**) is a government-recognized organization or individual that provides essential support and guidance to foreign entrepreneurs during their first year in the Netherlands under the Startup Visa program.

**Critical Requirement:** Working with a recognized facilitator is **mandatory** for obtaining a Netherlands Startup Visa.

## Why Facilitators Exist

The Dutch government created the facilitator system to:
- Ensure startups receive professional guidance
- Increase success rates of foreign entrepreneurs
- Provide quality control for the startup visa program
- Connect entrepreneurs with the Dutch business ecosystem
- Verify the viability and innovation of business ideas

## Facilitator's Core Responsibilities

### 1. **Pre-Application Phase**
- **Initial screening** of your business idea
- **Assessment** of innovation potential
- **Review** of your business plan
- **Decision** whether to accept you as a client

### 2. **Application Phase**
- **Co-signing** your business plan
- **Submitting** your visa application to IND (they can do this on your behalf)
- **Verification** of documentation
- **Communication** with RVO and IND

### 3. **During Your Startup Visa Year**
- **Regular mentorship** meetings
- **Operational support** (marketing, management, strategy)
- **Network access** (investors, customers, partners)
- **Office space** (some facilitators provide physical workspace)
- **Legal and administrative** guidance
- **Investor connections** and fundraising support
- **Market entry** assistance

### 4. **Transition to Self-Employment Permit**
- **Progress assessment** at year-end
- **Signing declaration** that exempts you from the points system (if satisfied with your progress)
- **Recommendation letter** for your self-employment visa application

## Legal Requirements for Facilitators

To be recognized by RVO, facilitators must meet strict criteria:

### ✅ **Required Qualifications**

1. **Experience:** Demonstrated track record in guiding innovative startups
2. **Financial Stability:** Proof of solvency and financial health
3. **No Bankruptcy:** Cannot be in receivership or bankruptcy proceedings
4. **No Negative Equity:** Must have positive financial standing
5. **Independence:** Cannot have majority interest in your startup company
6. **Not Family:** Cannot be related to you up to the third degree (child, parent, grandparent, aunt/uncle)
7. **Designated Deputy:** Must have a backup person in the organization

### 🔍 **RVO Verification**
RVO regularly audits facilitators to ensure they maintain these standards.

## How to Choose the Right Facilitator

### Step 1: **Identify Your Needs**
Consider:
- **Industry/sector:** Is there a sector-specific facilitator?
- **Stage:** Pre-seed, seed, or scaling?
- **Location preference:** Which Dutch city do you prefer?
- **Support type:** Do you need workspace, funding, technical expertise?
- **Network:** Do you need investor access, customer connections, or partnerships?

### Step 2: **Research Match**
For a software engineering startup, strong matches include:
- **Antler** - Global network, seed funding
- **CIC Rotterdam** - Tech innovation hub
- **Erasmus Centre** - Strong business foundation
- **HighTechXL** - Deep tech focus
- **Rockstart** - Tech scaling with VC backing
- **TNW Amsterdam** - Tech + media exposure
- **UtrechtInc** - Early-stage tech, university-backed

### Step 3: **Initial Contact**
Most facilitators want to see:
- **Pitch deck** (10-15 slides)
- **Brief business plan** or executive summary
- **Your background** and team
- **Traction** (if any) - users, revenue, partnerships

### Step 4: **Assessment Calls**
Typical process:
1. **First call:** Introduction, initial screening (30-45 min)
2. **Second call:** Detailed business discussion, meet potential mentor
3. **Decision:** Facilitator decides whether to accept you

### Step 5: **Negotiation**
Discuss:
- **Fees:** Typically €2,000 - €10,000+ per year
- **Equity:** Some take equity (5-10%), some don't
- **Services included:** Be specific about what support you'll receive
- **Commitment:** Meeting frequency, mentor availability
- **Success criteria:** What are the year-end expectations?

## Facilitator Fee Structures

### Common Models:

#### 1. **Fixed Fee Only**
- €3,000 - €7,000 per year
- No equity taken
- Defined service package

#### 2. **Equity-Based**
- 5-10% equity
- May include seed funding
- More intensive involvement

#### 3. **Hybrid**
- Small fee (€2,000 - €4,000)
- Plus small equity (3-5%)
- Balanced approach

#### 4. **Free/Subsidized**
- Some university-linked or government-backed facilitators
- Lower fees for certain programs
- May have restrictions

### What Fees Typically Cover:
✅ Visa application support
✅ Business plan review and co-signing
✅ Monthly mentorship meetings
✅ Network access (limited)
✅ Basic legal/admin guidance

### What May Cost Extra:
💰 Office space rental
💰 Intensive coaching beyond basic package
💰 Marketing/PR services
💰 Technical development support
💰 Investor introduction services

## Red Flags When Choosing a Facilitator

🚩 **Avoid if they:**
- Promise visa approval (they can't guarantee this)
- Have no verifiable track record
- Are not on the official RVO list
- Request payment before any discussion
- Take majority equity (>20%)
- Don't respond to your questions clearly
- Have no dedicated mentor assigned
- Don't ask detailed questions about your business
- Rush you into signing agreements

## The Facilitator Agreement

### Key Elements to Include:

#### 1. **Scope of Services**
```
- Specific mentorship (hours per month)
- Network access details
- Office space (if provided)
- Administrative support
- Duration (minimum 12 months)
```

#### 2. **Fees and Payment Terms**
```
- Base fee amount
- Payment schedule
- Equity stake (if any)
- Additional costs
- Refund policy (if any)
```

#### 3. **Responsibilities**
```
Your responsibilities:
- Active participation
- Monthly progress reports
- Meeting attendance
- Business development efforts

Their responsibilities:
- Mentorship provision
- Visa application support
- Network introductions
- Progress assessment
```

#### 4. **Termination Clauses**
```
- Under what conditions can either party exit?
- What happens to equity if you leave?
- Notice period required
```

#### 5. **Year-End Declaration**
```
- Criteria for successful completion
- Process for self-employment permit support
- What happens if they're unsatisfied with progress
```

## FAQ

### Q: Can I change facilitators during my visa year?
**A:** Technically possible but complicated. Requires new agreement and IND notification. Generally not recommended unless serious issues arise.

### Q: What if my facilitator goes bankrupt?
**A:** Your visa remains valid, but you'll need to find a new facilitator quickly. Contact IND and RVO immediately.

### Q: Can I have multiple facilitators?
**A:** No, you must designate one primary facilitator for the visa application.

### Q: Do I need to be in the Netherlands to meet facilitators?
**A:** No, most do initial meetings via video call. However, in-person meetings are preferred before final commitment.

### Q: How long does it take to get accepted by a facilitator?
**A:** Typically 2-6 weeks from first contact to signed agreement, depending on their schedule and your preparedness.

### Q: Can a facilitator refuse to sign my year-end declaration?
**A:** Yes, if they believe you haven't made sufficient progress. This means you'll need to apply for self-employment visa through the standard points system.

### Q: Are there facilitators who specialize in software/tech for Iranian founders?
**A:** No specific ethnic specialization, but tech-focused facilitators (CIC, UtrechtInc, TNW, Rockstart, Antler) have extensive experience with international founders from all backgrounds.

## Best Practices

### ✅ DO:
- Research 3-5 facilitators before deciding
- Prepare a solid pitch deck before reaching out
- Ask for references from their past clients
- Understand exactly what's included in the fee
- Get everything in writing
- Build a genuine relationship with your mentor
- Be proactive and responsive

### ❌ DON'T:
- Choose based on price alone
- Sign without reading the agreement carefully
- Expect them to build your business for you
- Miss scheduled meetings or check-ins
- Hide problems or challenges
- Wait until the last minute for year-end support
- Forget to leverage their network
